The Quest For 3%: Strategies to Improve H0 Precision

National Radio Astronomy Observatory REU (Charlottesville, VA)

The Megamaser Cosmology Project (MCP) aims to measure the Hubble Constant (H0) with high precision using water megamasers. The current measurement stands at 73.9 Â± 3.0 km s-1 Mpc-1, with an uncertainty of 4.06%. The goal is to reduce the uncertainty to 3%. This project investigates approaches such as refining disk-fitting models and re-observing certain galaxies to improve precision. By re-observing galaxies like NGC 5765b, uncertainty was reduced to 3.24%, demonstrating that the MCP is on course to achieve 3% precision.

Stellar Mass Function Evolution

Truman State University Research Project

This research project involved studying the evolution of the stellar mass function (SMF) over cosmic time. Using observational data from surveys, we aimed to quantify how the distribution of stellar masses in galaxies has changed from early cosmic epochs to the present day. By fitting the observed SMF data with theoretical models, we derived key insights into galaxy formation and evolution. The project explored how star formation, mergers, and other processes have influenced the mass distribution in galaxies.
